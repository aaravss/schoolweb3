Campus Life

Playing Field

Established in 1925

As well as a sport and PE venue, the Tanglin Playing Field is home to many recreational events, such as Lion Dances during Chinese New Year. A student favourite, the Astro is one of the most crowded places during break times and lunch! Here many sports events happen as well: football, cricket, rugby matches and many others take place in the Playing Field.



Senior Library

Established in 1930

The Senior Library is a vast area dedicated to helping our students learn and grow. Consisting of three seperate zones, the Interactive Zone, the Study Zone, and the Collaborative Zone, the students have a wide varitey of resources to help them with their academic needs. Many students also come here during lunch for a nice and quiet place to relax, or to partake in the many ongoing activities.



Senior Canteen

Established in 1927

The Senior Canteen provides our students with snacks and lucnhes during break times. A nice environment with hundreds of seats, the canteen is a popular place where students go during lunch and break. In the canteen students can pick what meals and snacks they would like, from Western to Asian lunches, or the many snacks available at the snack bar.



Junior and Infants Sport Hall

Established in 1999

The Junior and Infants Sport Hall is one of the many sports hall we have here at Tanglin. Some of the others would be the Berrick, Griffith-Jones, Smithson, Chandarana Sports Halls, each of which house important equipment for multiple differenet sports, from gymnastiques to badminton and basketball. Competeitons and Tournaments frequently take place in these halls.



Tanglin Pool

Established in 1935

The Tanglin Pool is an important part of the campus as swimming is a big thing in the school. The Tanglin Swimming team, known as the "Merlions" consist of some of the most talented young swimmers. However not only the Merlions use the pool, it is also a PE venue. Every Tanglin Student has swimming as one of their PE topics every year. We believe that swimming is an important skill for our students to learn.



School Achievements

Under 19 SEASAC Division 1 Girls Football
Won the 1st Division of Under 19 SEASAC Girls Football, 5 times in 5 years

Emma, the team's captain, has played a part in all five SEASAC victories, playing for the first time and scoring in the final when she was in Year 8. Now in Year 12 she was once again an outstanding player in the tournament, scoring a hat trick in the final. Emma had this to say: "SEASAC football is always the highlight of my year. This year was a tough tournament with a rough start on day one. Our team is one of the newest, yet strongest squads I have played for, with team members spanning over six year groups. It was such an achievement for all of us to keep the five year winning streak alive and we hope we can continue the tradition next year!"

Tanglin Trust School crowned Gold at Awards
Tanglin has been crowned Gold for Best UK Pathway at HoneyKids International School of the Year awards 2021.

Tanglin's win is testament of its quality education: each of Tanglin's schools are inspected every three years, following UK Ofsted British Schools Overseas (BSO) standards. The school has been repeatedly described by UK inspectors as Outstanding, the highest possible grade. This outcome is the result of carefully curated curriculum, which draws strongly from the rigour of the English National Curriculum, from Infant through Senior School. As one of the few not-for-profit schools in Singapore, all fees are devoted to providing an outstanding education to Tanglin's students.

Among the Top 5 schools in Singapore and Top 20 Globally
Tanglin Trust School has been listed amongst the top five independent schools in Singapore and top 20 independent schools worldwide based on International Baccalaureate (IB) results.

Beyond the IB programme, Tanglin is also the only International school in Singapore to offer A-level, allowing students the choose the educational pathway best suited for them. Tanglin's A-level results are equally as impressive with four students recently earning the prestigious Top in the World awards conferred by the Outstanding Cambridge Learner Awards (OCLA).

Tanglin highest overall achieving school in World Scholar's Cup
A World Renewed, a recount of the World Scholar's Cup, Regional Round.

Tanglin was the highest overall achieving school in the competition, with a spectacular 111 medals and commendations in total. Most impressively, Team Adam K, Krishiv K and Rohan S achieved the 1st place trophy for highest scoring Team in Senior Division, and Krishiv won the award for highest scoring individual scholar, as well as the recipient of the Asimov Award for best overall score in the Individual Quiz. Team Alicia F, Dishan K and Vittoria M came in 3rd overall team in the Senior Division. Zhai Z also achieved excellently, with 4th place overall in the Junior Division. Sharbani U, Anne Y and Samantha Y were awarded 1st place overall in Debate (Junior Division). All of our students achieved medals for excellent performances in particular areas, and I am very proud of all of them. Most importantly, they all had a fantastic time: the atmosphere was truly electric, and it was wonderful to see students engaging so excitedly and with such determination.
